What the test results mean to me

These test results show that I am an agreeable person putting the needs of others before my own (TRUITY n.d.). The low extroversion score is very understandable for me. I dislike putting any personal information about myself out into the open and prefer to talk to people face to face (TRUITY n.d.). It also explains why I would not have a face book profile if it didn’t help when looking for jobs. (TRUITY n.d.).

How do you think these results may influence my behaviour in a team?

While I work for others, I have low extroversion scores meaning that I don’t seek to socialise and find it mentally draining to talk to people (TRUITY n.d.). I am a visual learner and prioritise to learn new information and make conclusions based of logical deductions based on the knowledge I have acquired (NERIS Analytics Limited n.d.). While these scores talk about my personality I will have to be aware of the weaknesses and work to shore them up while also building upon my strengths (TRUITY n.d.).

How should you take the test results into account when forming a team?

The 16 personality and the big five personality test may identify me as an introvert this means that I have to be conscious to ensure there Is an method of communication for all of my team members. These results show that I can work in a calm and logical manor independently (NERIS Analytics Limited n.d.). My learning style is mostly a visual learner but as this test shows I am not only a visual learner I am a tactile and auditory learner (Pennsylvania Higher Education Assistance Agency n.d.). That being stated personally I believe that that work in a group can create results greater than the independent effort of just one person due to a team bringing everyone’s strengths together to cover out weaknesses. When forming a team, you should dedicate people into different roles that promotes their strengths (Suner 2020).
